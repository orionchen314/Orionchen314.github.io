Beyond academic research, I maintain a set of structured personal interests that contribute to my physical endurance, mental clarity, and problem-solving mindset.

### Cycling

![Road cycling](/assets/img/hobbies/road_cycling.jpg)

I am an active road cyclist and regularly engage in medium-to-long distance rides. Cycling serves both as cardiovascular training and as a way to build long-term discipline. I am particularly interested in performance tracking, pacing strategies, and endurance optimization.

I am trained across both road cycling and mountain biking, and I hold a National Level-2 Referee certification for cycling and triathlon events. This experience gives me a structured understanding of race organization, rules, and performance evaluation.

### Fitness Training

I follow a structured strength training program (push–pull–legs split) with an emphasis on muscle hypertrophy, lower-body strength, and injury-aware training. Given my previous knee surgery, I pay particular attention to stability, recovery, and progressive loading.

In addition to personal training, I have practical experience in identifying and managing sports-related injuries, particularly knee-related issues common in cycling.

### Reading

My reading interests span literature, philosophy, and scientific writing. I am especially drawn to works that explore human cognition, existential themes, and the relationship between science and society.

### Technology and Coding

I actively explore programming, data analysis, and system-level problem solving outside of formal research. This includes working with C++, Python, ROOT, and data-processing pipelines, often applied to experimental physics contexts.

### Travel and Exploration

I enjoy planning and executing independent travel, with a focus on experiencing diverse environments and cultures. Travel, for me, is closely tied to observation, adaptability, and real-world learning.

### Experimental and Engineering Projects

I have a strong interest in hands-on system building, including detector instrumentation, electronics, and experimental setups. I enjoy translating theoretical ideas into working physical systems and optimizing them through iterative testing.

### Community and Teaching

I have been actively involved in student organizations and sports communities. I organized and delivered a lecture titled "How to Identify and Manage Knee Injuries in Cycling" as part of a cycling association workshop series. The session covered injury mechanisms, self-assessment methods, and practical response strategies.

![Cycling injury workshop](/assets/img/hobbies/knee_injury_lecture.jpg)

I have also served as a team medical support member (team doctor role) in university cycling clubs, where I provided guidance on injury prevention, recovery strategies, and on-site assessment during training and activities.

Beyond academic research, my interests are not isolated activities but form a coherent system that shapes how I approach endurance, engineering, and problem solving. They can be roughly grouped into three domains: **physical discipline**, **intellectual exploration**, and **practical engineering**.

---

## 1. Physical Discipline

### Cycling

![Road cycling](/assets/img/hobbies/road_cycling.jpg)

Cycling is my primary long-term commitment. I regularly engage in medium-to-long distance road cycling, focusing on endurance, pacing strategies, and performance optimization.

I am trained in both road cycling and mountain biking, and I hold a **National Level-2 Referee certification** for cycling and triathlon events. This provides not only athletic experience but also a structured understanding of race systems, rules, and performance evaluation.

### Fitness Training

I follow a structured **push–pull–legs strength training program**, with emphasis on:
- Muscle hypertrophy  
- Lower-body strength  
- Injury-aware progression  

Due to a previous knee surgery, I place strong emphasis on **stability, recovery, and load control**, which has led me to develop a systematic approach to injury prevention and rehabilitation.

---

## 2. From Practice to Knowledge

### Injury Awareness & Teaching

![Cycling injury workshop](/assets/img/hobbies/knee_injury_lecture.jpg)

My experience in sports naturally extended into injury analysis and community education.

I organized and delivered a lecture:

**“How to Identify and Manage Knee Injuries in Cycling”**

This workshop focused on:
- Mechanisms of common cycling injuries  
- Self-assessment methods  
- Practical response and recovery strategies  

In addition, I have served in a **team medical support role** within university cycling clubs, providing guidance on injury prevention and on-site assessment during training.

This reflects a transition from **individual practice → systematic understanding → community contribution**.

---

## Summary

These activities are not independent hobbies but form an integrated structure:

- **Cycling and training** build discipline and physical resilience  
- **Injury analysis and teaching** extend practice into structured knowledge  
- **Reading and coding** strengthen abstract and analytical thinking  
- **Engineering projects** connect theory with real-world implementation  
- **Travel** enhances adaptability and observation  

Together, they reinforce a consistent approach:  
**systematic thinking applied across physical, intellectual, and technical domains**
